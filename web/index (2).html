Common Misconceptions

I'm too late! You have to start very early for second language learning or you will miss the boat
Although young children may seem to pick up language quickly, older children can also master second languages, all that matters is time and effort put into learning the language.
Will my bilingual child mix up languages?
Mixing languages is temporary and goes away as the child's language vocabulary increases
Only native speakers and teachers can teach children second languages?
As long as children are getting exposure to the language and are interacting with adults, the child will be able to pick up the language and use it effectively.
Two languages are the most a very young child should be exposed to
The more the merrier, if the child has an opportunity to engage in meaningful interaction in each language on a daily basis, THEN GO FOR IT!

Children who are Bilingual:

In Cognitive Development

  • Have gained literacy skills
  • Focus on tasks which will help in being successful in school
  • Think outside of the box and come up with multiple solutions when they are faced with a problem than those who are monolingual
  • Tend to score better on test than monolingual children.

In Health and Social

  • Have reduced levels of anxiety
  • Maintain strong ties with their family, culture and community
  • Have higher self-esteem
  • Are translator which allows the child to practice the language through verbal interaction

In Global Perspective

  • Are more accepting of others from different cultures
  • Have a higher and better chance of getting high paying jobs in the future
  • Open to participating in community activities
  • Enjoy exploring different cultural backgrounds
  • Can help assist others around the world
